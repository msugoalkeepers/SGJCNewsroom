⚠ Deadline: March 25, 2026 — Due tomorrow. Editor review pending.

University athletics officials confirmed Tuesday that the department will face a 15 percent budget reduction beginning next fall, a move administrators say is necessary to address broader financial shortfalls affecting multiple divisions.

The cuts, which were quietly discussed in a January board meeting, would eliminate two junior varsity programs and reduce coaching staff by at least four full-time positions, according to three sources familiar with the deliberations who spoke on the condition of anonymity.

What's at stake

Head basketball coach Derek Monroe said his program would lose two assistant coaching positions under the proposed plan. "We're being asked to do more with less, and I'm not sure that's sustainable for our athletes," Monroe told The Pressroom in an interview Wednesday.

"The students who came here expecting a full athletic experience deserve better than this. We need answers, not budget lines." — student-athlete, junior

The women's volleyball and men's cross-country programs are among those facing potential elimination, sources said. The university's athletic director did not respond to multiple requests for comment.

Administrative response

Vice Provost for Finance Diane Chu said in a statement that the university is "exploring all options to maintain competitive programs while meeting our fiscal responsibilities." She declined to confirm specific cuts.

The Student Government Association has called for an emergency town hall with university leadership, set for next Thursday at 5 p.m. in the Student Union.
