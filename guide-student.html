Goalkeepers Pressroom · Quick-Start Guide

Welcome to the Newsroom

Everything you need to start writing, submitting stories, and collaborating with your team — in one place, from any browser.

Getting Started

Signing In

You don't need to create an account. Just sign in with your Google account — the same one you use for university email — and your editor will approve you.

First-Time Sign In
1
Open your browser and go to: https://msugoalkeepers.github.io/SGJCNewsroom/
2
Click "Continue with Google" and sign in with your university Google account.
3
You'll see an "Access Pending" screen. This is normal — your editor needs to approve you first.
4
Once approved, you'll get a notification and can log in to see your assignments.
⚠If you see "Access Removed" instead of "Access Pending" — your account was deactivated. Contact your editor-in-chief directly.

Writing

The Story Editor

Click any assignment to open the Story Editor. If you're co-assigned with other reporters, all of you can write and submit. Your work saves automatically every 2 seconds — you will never lose content.

Writing Your Story
1
Type your headline at the top. The deck (subheadline) goes in the second field.
2
Write in the main body area. The word count bar fills toward your target and turns green when you hit it.
3
Use the toolbar for Bold, Italic, Headings, Pull Quotes, and Links.
4
Click ✓ Grammar in the toolbar to check spelling and grammar (see below).
5
Add sources, leave comments, and attach media using the panels on the right side.
6
When ready, click "Submit for Review." Your editor is notified instantly.
💾Your story autosaves every 2 seconds. The "Saved" indicator in the top right confirms it. You will never lose your work.
Writing

Grammar & Spelling Check

Click the ✓ Grammar button in the story editor toolbar at any time. The app sends your text to a grammar checking service and returns a list of issues grouped by type.

  • Each issue shows the error type, a description, and the sentence in context with the problem word highlighted in red
  • Click any suggestion button under an issue to apply the fix directly in your story
  • After applying a fix, the check runs again automatically
  • Browser spell check is always on — red underlines appear in real time as you type, just like in a word processor
💡Run the grammar check right before clicking "Submit for Review." Catching errors early saves your editor time and reflects well on your work.

Writing

Comments & Reactions

The Comments panel (right side of editor) is for back-and-forth with your editor. Use it to ask questions, flag concerns, or respond to feedback.

  • Type your comment and press Enter or click Send
  • Click the ＋ button under any comment to add a reaction: ❤ 👍 👎. Click it again to remove.
  • Use the ✎ button to edit your own comment after posting
  • Use the ✕ button to delete your own comment
  • If an editor leaves a note in red in your story body (Editor Markup), click ⇄ Compare in the Version History panel to see exactly what changed

Adding Media

Photos and media are added via Google Drive links — no file uploads needed.

Add a Photo from Google Drive
1
Upload your photo to Google Drive.
2
Right-click the file → Share → change to "Anyone with the link" → Copy link.
3
In your story, click the ⊡ Photo button in the toolbar and paste the link.
4
Or, in the Media panel on the right, click "+ Add" and paste any link (YouTube, Drive, SoundCloud, etc.).

Writing

Submitting for Review

When your story is ready, click "Submit for Review" in the top right of the story editor. Any reporter co-assigned to the story can submit it.

  • Your editor receives an instant notification
  • The story status changes from Draft to In Review
  • If your editor sends it back, you'll get a notification and it returns to Draft
  • When it's approved, it becomes Published and moves to the Archive — read-only

Other Features

Pitch Board

Have a story idea? Go to Pitch Board in the sidebar and click "+ Submit Pitch."

  • Fill in your working headline, section, story type, and idea brief
  • Add the sources you plan to interview and whether you have access
  • Set a proposed deadline
  • Your editor will Approve, Hold, or Pass — you get a notification either way
  • Approved pitches can be converted directly into a full assignment

Other Features

Calendar

The calendar shows assignment deadlines, U.S. holidays, World Cup 2026 fixtures, and Black SoccerLab events all in one view.

  • Use the filter pills below the navigation bar to show only one category at a time
  • Orange = Assignment deadlines · Purple = U.S. Holidays · Dark green = World Cup 2026 · Black = Black SoccerLab Events
  • Click any event to see full details
  • Any team member can click "+ Add Event" to add a Black SoccerLab Event or custom entry
  • Recurring events show a ↻ symbol on each occurrence

Quick Tips

  • Font size: drag the A– A+ slider in the top bar to make text larger or smaller — it saves your preference
  • Version history: every significant save creates a version — click Restore to go back to any of them
  • Story diff: when a story is In Review, click ⇄ Compare in the Version History panel to see your draft vs the editor's version side by side
  • Read receipts: your editor can see when you first opened your assignment
  • Deadline reminders: an amber bar appears on the Dashboard when any of your stories are due within 48 hours
  • Notifications: the bell icon shows all recent notifications — click any to act on it
🆘Need help? Contact your editor-in-chief. For technical issues, press F12 → Console, take a screenshot of any red errors, and share them.
